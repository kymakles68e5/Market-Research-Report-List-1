<p><p>The automotive windscreen market comprises several types, including laminated glass, toughened glass, and other materials. Laminated glass features a sandwich of two glass layers with a polyvinyl butyral interlayer, offering enhanced safety and noise reduction. Toughened glass, or tempered glass, is heat-treated for increased strength and shatter resistance, commonly used in side and rear windows. Other materials may include polycarbonate and specialized coatings, catering to niche applications or providing additional functionalities like UV protection and self-cleaning properties.</p></p>

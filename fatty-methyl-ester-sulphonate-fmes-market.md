The FMES market can be segmented into two main types: esterification and sulphonation. Esterification involves the reaction of fatty acids with methanol to produce esters, while sulphonation refers to the addition of sulfonic acid groups to the ester, enhancing its surfactant properties. Together, these processes create FMES, which is valued for its environmental benefits and effectiveness in various formulations.</p></p>
